# Deep-Space Convergence

## Intent

A compact pulsar, a distant quasar, and one ceremonial spacecraft share a single readable deep-space vista. The pulsar remains the dominant foreground subject; the quasar establishes cosmic distance, while the small vessel supplies human scale.

## Composition

The cyan neutron star sits off-axis in the near field and sweeps two tilted directional beams through nested magnetic rings. A smaller gold-violet quasar recedes into the opposite corner with a bright accretion crown and opposing jets. The spacecraft follows a smooth bounded route through the negative space between them. Sparse local stars, contrasting scale, translucent halos, and the shared far starfield create depth without hiding the silhouettes.

## Interaction and constraints

- Space/Click pulse brightens both energetic sources, widens their jets, and strengthens the spacecraft engine response.
- Pulsar rotation is deliberately much faster than the patient quasar drift; spacecraft translation and roll remain smooth and bounded.
- Reduced motion freezes continuous rotation and travel at a composed resting pose while pulse feedback remains visible.
- Low and medium quality reduce local stars and radial segments; no external assets, runtime fetches, shadows, or per-frame allocations are used.
- The pulsar is the hero. The quasar and spacecraft must remain clearly distinguishable but subordinate, preserving broad dark negative space.
