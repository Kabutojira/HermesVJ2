# Monolith Studio chapter brief

## Composition

One original procedural monolith is the hero, framed off-axis against a charcoal cyclorama. Its lathed silhouette transitions from a planted base through a subtle shoulder to a narrow crown. A small warm aperture and collar provide human-readable scale and controlled contrast without resembling a branded product.

## Materials and light

The hero uses brushed, clear-coated metal with enough roughness to preserve edge definition. Broad rectangular key, fill, and rim lights produce long softbox highlights. The dark physical floor is metallic but rough, so it carries wide, blurred lighting reflections rather than a perfect mirror.

## Effects and quality

The shared high-tier-only post chain applies very restrained bloom to the aperture and slight depth of field focused at the hero distance. Medium, low, and reduced-motion modes retain the same composition without post processing. Geometry segments, shadowing, clearcoat detail, and floor finish adapt to quality. No external assets are required.
